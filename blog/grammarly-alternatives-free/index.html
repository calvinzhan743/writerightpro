5 Free Grammarly Alternatives That Actually Work in 2026

Are you a writer, remote worker, or content creator looking for a reliable grammar-checking tool? While Grammarly remains a popular choice, it can be expensive for some users. The good news is that there are several free alternatives that can help you polish your writing without breaking the bank. In this article, we’ll explore five free Grammarly alternatives that actually work in 2026—plus a few premium recommendations for those who want to take their writing to the next level.

1. ProWritingAid

ProWritingAid is a robust grammar checker that offers a free version with comprehensive features. Whether you’re crafting a blog post, essay, or professional email, ProWritingAid analyzes your writing for grammar, style, and readability. It’s an excellent alternative for writers who need detailed suggestions to improve their work. While the free version has some limitations, it’s perfect for occasional users or those on a budget.

2. Hemingway Editor

The Hemingway Editor is a unique tool that focuses on simplicity and clarity. It highlights complex sentences, passive voice, and adverbs, helping you write concise and impactful content. The desktop version is free, making it a fantastic option for writers who want to refine their style without spending a dime. If you’re crafting content for the web, Hemingway Editor is particularly useful for ensuring your writing is easy to read.

3. LanguageTool

LanguageTool is an open-source grammar checker that supports over 20 languages, making it ideal for multilingual writers. Its free version detects grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors, while the premium version offers advanced style suggestions. LanguageTool is a great alternative for content creators who need a versatile and user-friendly tool. Plus, it integrates seamlessly with browsers, Google Docs, and Microsoft Word.

4. Scribens

Scribens is another free grammar checker that offers detailed corrections and explanations. It’s particularly useful for writers who want to understand their mistakes and learn as they go. Scribens highlights grammar, spelling, and style issues, making it a solid choice for students and professionals alike. While it doesn’t have as many features as Grammarly, it’s a reliable and cost-effective solution.
